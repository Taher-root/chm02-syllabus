About This Course

What is CHM-02?

CHM-02 introduces the fundamental principles of chemistry — atomic theory, bonding, molecular structure, reactions, stoichiometry, gases, acids, bases, and solutions. It emphasizes problem-solving and prepares you for CHEM-01A.

You don't have to be a "chemistry person" already. This course is built to get you there.

My Teaching Philosophy

How I approach teaching — and learning

I believe every student can learn chemistry when they're met with respect, clear guidance, and genuine support. My job isn't to weed people out — it's to help you build understanding step by step and to remove the obstacles that get in the way of learning.

I'll keep the course organized and transparent, so you always know what we're doing and why. I'll explain ideas in more than one way, welcome your questions, and check in when I sense you could use support. I see mistakes — yours and mine — as a normal, useful part of learning, not as failures.

Most of all, I want this classroom to be a place where you feel you belong and where your effort pays off. If something isn't working for you, tell me, and we'll figure it out together. Your success is my goal, and I'll do my part to help you reach it.

Our Agreement

What we commit to each other

A course works best when everyone knows what to expect. These are our shared commitments.

What you can expect of me

  1. I will treat you with dignity and respect and be flexible to support your individual needs.
  2. I will provide a clear, organized course designed to ensure you meet our course outcomes in a meaningful way.

  5. You will give yourself grace. Expect to make mistakes; you are human, and mistakes are part of learning and growing.

Course Policies

How this course works

These policies are designed to support your success — not to trip you up. When in doubt, reach out.

Homework — effort-based

Homework is mostly effort-based: attempt and complete it and you'll earn most of the credit (I spot-check for completion and accuracy). It's one of the best ways to prepare for quizzes and exams. If something doesn't click, please ask me, a classmate, or a tutor — seeking help is exactly the right move.

Exams

Because chemistry builds on itself, exams are cumulative, and all dates are shared up front so you can plan ahead. If you have an unavoidable, documented situation and let me know in advance, I'll work with you on options such as an early exam.

Academic Integrity

Academic integrity makes grades meaningful and keeps our learning environment fair. Please do your work honestly and independently unless collaboration is clearly allowed. If you're ever unsure what help, collaboration, citation, or technology (including AI tools) is permitted — ask before the due date. Asking is always welcome. If I have a concern, I'll talk with you first and follow college procedures.

Attendance & Communication

Attending regularly really helps you succeed, and I know life happens. If something may affect your participation — work, health, transportation, caregiving, tech — reach out as early as you can and we'll find options together. You don't need to share private details, just enough for me to help.

Our Classroom Community

We share responsibility for a respectful, inclusive space where everyone can focus and participate. Treat one another with kindness, give others room to contribute, and use devices in ways that support learning. Every student has the right to learn free from discrimination and harassment.
